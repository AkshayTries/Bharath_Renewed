According to the Central Statistical Institute of India, there were 3.3 million NGOs registered in India as of 2009. That is one NGO for every 400 citizens. Still, India faces so many social and economic challenges. Several charity organizations work to address these issues. In this blog, we will introduce you to the top 10 genuine charity organizations in India. These NGOs have demonstrated their dedication to various causes. Through their dedicated service, they have earned the trust of countless individuals and communities.



India's nonprofit sector is key in addressing a wide range of social and humanitarian challenges. Many NGOs work to provide education and healthcare. Some other NGOs work to alleviate poverty and conserve the environment. Individuals like you and corporations support these NGOs in their noble causes by donating frequently.

1. Team Everest
Team Everest's mission is to inspire everyone to volunteer and create a culture of giving. They do this by creating volunteering opportunities throughout the year, where anyone from the age of 3 to 95 can volunteer. Team Everest is recognized as one of India's leading NGOs committed to promoting volunteering and making it a way of life. For 17 years, they have been at the forefront of promoting volunteering through initiatives aligned with the Sustainability Development Goals, blending a professional approach with genuine passion.



Team Everest operates in 12 cities - Bangalore, Chennai, Coimbatore, Delhi, Gurgaon, Hyderabad, Kochi, Kolkata, Madurai, Mumbai, Noida, and Pune. Team Everest has conducted many corporate volunteering programs for 250+ corporate partners including IBM, Ford, Tata, Freshworks, etc. The NGO also provides scholarships to college students, with a commitment to making education accessible to all.

2. CRY (Child Rights and You)
CRY is an NGO dedicated to protecting the rights of children. They work to end child labour and improve access to education and healthcare. They advocate for policies that protect the well-being of India's underprivileged children. CRY addresses needs like healthcare, nutrition, education, and protection from child labour and child marriage.



Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra
Year Founded: 1979﻿
3. Smile Foundation
Smile Foundation is an NGO working for the welfare of children. This organization commits to providing education, healthcare, and support to orphans and underprivileged children. Some of their notable programs include Smile on Wheels, Mission Education, and Smile Twin e-learning.



Location: New Delhi
Year Founded: 2002﻿
4. GiveIndia Foundation
Give India Foundation is India's largest donation platform. It serves as a vital bridge between donors and many NGOs nationwide. Their mission encompasses diverse causes, including education, healthcare, and environmental initiatives. This non-profit organization operates as an online donation platform. They connect individuals from India and around the world to trusted NGOs. It enables donors to make meaningful contributions. Through their website, GiveIndia raises and distributes funds.



Location: Bengaluru
Year Founded: 1999﻿
5. HelpAge India
HelpAge India is an organization committed to enhancing the lives of elderly citizens. They provide essential healthcare services, support livelihoods, and advocate for the well-being and dignity of senior citizens. The organization's mission includes improving the living standards of underprivileged elderly individuals. HelpAge India takes action against pension disparities, inadequate healthcare access, and elder abuse. They have age-specific care programs to address the needs of disadvantaged elders.



Location: New Delhi
Year Founded: 1978﻿
6. Akshaya Patra Foundation
The Akshaya Patra Foundation provides mid-day meals to schoolchildren. The Akshaya Patra Foundation dedicates itself to tackling hunger in classrooms. Their mission is to supply nutritious meals to school children. They also support the Right to Education of children coming from challenging backgrounds.



Location: Bengaluru, Karnataka
Year Founded: 2000﻿
7. Goonj
Goonj NGO is an organization combating clothing poverty and promoting rural development. The organization collects surplus clothing and essential materials and distributes them to underprivileged communities. Moreover, Goonj's work extends to disaster relief, humanitarian aid and community development.



Location: New Delhi
Year Founded: 1999﻿
8. Project Nanhi Kali
Nanhi Kali's primary mission is to empower underprivileged girl children through education. They offer comprehensive support, including academic help, essential resources, and mentorship programs. They aim to help girls overcome obstacles and complete their education. The Naandi Foundation and KC Mahindra Education Trust manage this initiative. Through Project Nani Kali, the organization strives to educate and influence girls and women.



Location: Mumbai
Year Founded: 1996﻿
9. SERUDS
SERUDS NGO India is a dedicated organization committed to the betterment of marginalized communities. They strive to make a positive impact through a comprehensive range of programs, including education, healthcare, livelihood development, and assistance for vulnerable groups like children, women, and the elderly. By focusing on these key areas, SERUDS plays a crucial role in addressing the multifaceted challenges faced by underserved people, ultimately working towards a more equitable and inclusive society.



Location: Kurnool, Andhra Pradesh
Year Founded: 2003
10. Pratham Education Foundation
Pratham Education Foundation focuses on providing quality education to financially poor children. They started by providing education to children in the slums of Mumbai. They are one of the largest NGOs in the country focusing on high-quality, low-cost programs to address gaps in the education system. From early childhood education to vocational training, they work directly with communities and in partnership with governments to bring children to school.



Location: New Delhi
Year Founded: 1995
